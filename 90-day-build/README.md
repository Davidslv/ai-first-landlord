# 90-Day Build Checklist

The per-week tick-box version of the build sequence. Chapter 18 is the narrative; this folder is the workable form you tick along.

**Canonical reference:** Chapter 18 (The 90-Day Build) and Appendix G (90-Day Build Checklist).

---

## Who this is for

Calibrated to the working portfolio landlord with **four to twelve properties**, already let. Above twelve or HMO-heavy runs four to six months; two or three properties compress but rarely below sixty days.

**Total active build:** roughly 60–90 hours. Of that, around 20–30 hours is *net additional* time you cannot reclaim from existing routines — concentrated in Phase 2.

If you cannot commit 20–30 hours of net additional time across the 90 days, the plan slips to 120–150 days. That is workable. What does not work is compressing Phase 2 to catch up — the substrate (Property Knowledge Files, TOMs, voice profile) is what every later phase reads from.

## How to use the checklist

1. **Read [`checklist.md.txt`](checklist.md.txt) end-to-end before starting.** The phase boundaries matter.
2. **Tick before Day 1** — four boxes acknowledging the scope and the substrate-first rule.
3. **Date the Monday box at the start of each week.** Tick each day's tasks as you complete them.
4. **Do not skip phase boundaries.** End of Phase 1 → review outputs, mark complete in your voice/README.md training log.
5. **Slippage rule:** if a line slips a week, the plan slips a week — do not compress Phase 2 to catch up.

## Phase summary

| Phase | Days | Hours | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 — Foundation** | 1–7 | ~5–7 | Two folders in inbox, business-tier vendor subscribed, one property + one tenancy chosen as worked example, substrate chapters read. |
| **Phase 2 — Substrate** | 8–30 | ~25–35 | Property Knowledge File for every property; TOM for every active tenancy (internal + AI-submission versions); Voice Profile trained and saved. |
| **Phase 3 — Operational layer** | 31–60 | ~15–20 | Daily Inbox Brief running every morning; five-bucket categorisation installed; drafting workflow live; first 5 templates seeded. |
| **Phase 4 — Workflow Engine** | 61–80 | ~10–15 | Compliance dashboard live with standing rules; re-letting workflow ready; onboarding workflow ready. |
| **Phase 5 — MTD + Audit** | 81–90 | ~8–12 | MTD ITSA wired (if applicable); bookkeeper handoff agreed; dry submission against the most recent closed quarter; first quarterly bottleneck-finding pass; calibration dry-runs. |

---

## Slippage — the recovery rule

**Do not skip the substrate.** Compressing Phase 2 makes the system fragile.

- **Phase 1 slip:** extend up to a week.
- **Phase 2 slip:** extend up to two weeks; do not start Phase 3 against incomplete substrate.
- **Phase 3 slip:** extend up to a week; brief at 9/10 before Phase 4.
- **Phase 4 slip:** extend up to a week; dashboard live before Phase 5 MTD wiring.
- **Phase 5 slip:** extend up to two weeks; dry submission and bottleneck pass are the load-bearing outputs, not the day count.

## What you have on Day 90

The Day 90 completion check sits at the foot of [`checklist.md.txt`](checklist.md.txt). Headline outputs:

- Two folders in the inbox; inbox is no longer the system.
- Property Knowledge File for every property (HMO-variant where it applies).
- TOM for every active tenancy, in internal and AI-submission versions.
- Voice Profile producing drafts you recognise as your own writing.
- Daily Inbox Brief at 7:00 every morning.
- Five-bucket categorisation, Friday five-minute audit on the calendar.
- Drafting workflow with a 60–90 second three-question review pass.
- Five templates seeded from concluded correspondence.
- Compliance dashboard drafting its own reminders 8 weeks out.
- Re-letting workflow ready, dry-run against historic data.
- Onboarding workflow ready, dry-run against the last move-in.
- MTD ITSA cadence wired (if in scope); bookkeeper handoff agreed; dry submission completed.
- First quarterly bottleneck-finding pass complete; next pass on the calendar 90 days out.

Day 90 produces a **working system, not a finished product.** Refinement runs forever; the audit cadence (quarterly bottleneck pass; annual revalidation) keeps it sharp.
