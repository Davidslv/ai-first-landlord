## Why this entry is the canonical S13 shape

- **Pegs the dashboard chase to a recorded decision.** When the bookkeeper queries the rent figure on the next quarterly MTD pull, the answer is one lookup away.
- **The rationale field cites comparables.** A challenge at the First-tier Tribunal (Property Chamber) would test the proposed rent against the local open-market range; the decision-log entry preserves the comparable evidence at the time of the decision.
- **The percentage uplift is named.** Tribunal practice considers proportionality; recording the uplift makes it visible.
- **The chase open date is referenced.** The dashboard fires eight weeks before the service deadline (which is two months before the anniversary), and the decision-log entry should be created before the chase fires — not after the form is served.
- **Outcome is recorded against tenant acceptance** rather than Tribunal challenge. A challenged Section 13 with a Tribunal market-rent decision would carry a different outcome line: *Resolved [DD/MM/YYYY]. Tribunal set market rent at £[X] effective [DD/MM/YYYY].*
