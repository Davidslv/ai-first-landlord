## Why this entry matters

A missed Section 13 cycle is **never an unrecorded slip**. Either the dashboard fired all four chase points and the human did not act (this entry's pattern), or the dashboard failed to fire (a substrate problem — different kind of audit finding).

This entry's discipline:

- **Names the chase points fired.** Eight-week, four-week, and one-week openings are evidenced — the dashboard's success metric is whether the failure was visible at every chase point, not whether the action was taken.
- **Names the competing priority** that displaced the cover-note draft. *Information Sheet rollout, Q1 MTD ITSA preparation* is the kind of context an Ombudsman or Tribunal panel can read as a working landlord rather than indifference.
- **Considers and rejects the recovery option.** A late-cycle service for a non-anniversary-aligned effective date is technically available; this entry records why it was rejected (small uplift, precedent risk).
- **Sets the next-cycle baseline.** The Year+1 chase open date and service deadline are recorded — the audit trail for the next cycle starts before the next cycle's chase fires.

A Section 13 missed-cycle entry without the Recovery / Outcome fields is an open item; the quarterly bottleneck pass surfaces any such open items.
