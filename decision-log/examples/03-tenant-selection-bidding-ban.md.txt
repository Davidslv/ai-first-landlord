## Why this entry's discipline matters

The RRA 2025 bidding ban makes **rejection rationales load-bearing**. Three things keep the decision-log entry short and strong:

- **The applicant log is the load-bearing artefact.** All applicants timestamped, with criteria-pass/fail and rejection rationale per applicant, kept on file. The decision-log entry is the cover document that names it.
- **Criteria applied uniformly, named in the rationale.** Affordability with the gross-income floor; Right to Rent method; prior landlord reference; reference-agency credit/conduct. Same four criteria for every applicant.
- **The tie-break rule is recorded.** First-applied-first-considered by timestamp removes any subjective ordering that would re-introduce bidding-by-the-back-door.
- **What is *not* in the criteria** is named: *no applicant rejected on grounds of children or benefits.* The Equality Act 2010 plus post-RRA anti-discrimination provisions combine; the entry pre-empts a discrimination claim by recording uniformity on the protected characteristics.

A rejection — declining the first qualifying applicant — would carry a longer rationale field naming the specific criterion failed and citing the reference report or income calculation. *Reject application from [APPLICANT_REF]: gross household income £[X] below the £[FLOOR] affordability floor (30× rent).*
