## Why this entry's discipline matters

The 28-day window under RRA 2025 is hard-edged: **silence past 28 days deems consent given**, which means a refusal served on day 29 is invalid. The entry's three load-bearing details:

- **The request date is logged alongside the decision date.** *Day [N] of the 28-day window* in the outcome field proves response inside the statutory window.
- **The head lease / building rule is cited.** A leasehold flat may carry a head-lease pet clause that constrains what the landlord can permit; freeholders may pre-empt or override the landlord's consent. The decision-log entry pre-empts a freeholder dispute by recording the head-lease check.
- **Conditions track a published template.** The NRLA pet-permission template is a defendable starting point — *conditions match the NRLA pet-permission template (current year update)* signals the conditions are not invented case by case.

**A refusal would carry a longer rationale field.** Grounds must be reasonable and recorded:

- *Refused: [PET_TYPE] [SPECIFIC_GROUND — e.g. species/breed not permitted under the freeholder's insurance, structural damage risk specific to the property, etc].*
- Cite the underlying source (insurance certificate; surveyor letter; freeholder direction).
- Cross-reference any earlier refusal pattern that might be construed as unreasonable across the portfolio.

The PETS file is the place where a pattern of refusals is visible at audit — repeated rejections without comparable rationale is a flag for the Chapter 16 quarterly pass.
