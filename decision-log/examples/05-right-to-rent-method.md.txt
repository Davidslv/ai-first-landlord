## Why this entry's discipline matters

The Right to Rent check is a **landlord act**, not an AI act. The decision-log entry records *which method and why*, plus the document group — three pieces that together protect against a £10,000 first-breach civil penalty per occupier (£20,000 on repeat).

Three load-bearing details:

- **The method chosen and the alternatives considered.** *Manual physical / IDVT / Home Office online* are the three permitted routes; the entry records which one and why one was preferred over the others. For British and Irish citizens, both manual and IDVT are valid — the IDSP fee versus parity-with-portfolio trade-off is a recordable judgement.
- **The document group.** List A vs List B; Group 1 vs Group 2. List A Group 1 (UK passport, Irish passport in date) means **no re-check required**. List A Group 2 or List B documents may carry time limits and so a re-check date.
- **The re-check status and date** (where applicable). Time-limited statuses generate a re-check row on the dashboard; Group 1 documents go *N/A — Group 1*. The entry pre-populates the dashboard's R2R column.

**Edge cases for the rationale field:**

- A non-UK / non-Irish applicant whose IDVT is not valid for them — record why manual or share-code was the only available method.
- A BRP holder whose card has a time-limited expiry shorter than the tenancy — re-check date is the BRP expiry date, not the tenancy end date.
- An IDSP-mediated check where the IDSP raised a flag — record the flag, the resolution, and any escalation to Home Office guidance.

A failed Right to Rent check (no qualifying document, time-limited status with no re-check feasible) would carry a different decision: *Decline tenancy grant: [APPLICANT_REF] unable to evidence Right to Rent under Immigration Act 2014. Application not progressed.* That entry would cross-reference the SELECT log because the failed RTR is also the rejection rationale.
