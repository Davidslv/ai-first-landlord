## Why this entry's discipline matters

The voluntary-adoption framing is a **decision about standards above the floor**. A landlord operating only at the s.11 LTA 1985 / Homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018 / HHSRS minimum is technically compliant pre-Awaab's-Law commencement; voluntarily adopting the social-housing timetable is a defendable standing rule.

Three load-bearing details:

- **The standing rule is named.** *Inspection within 14 days, emergency repair within 24 hours, significant repair within 7 days.* This is the social-housing timetable lifted into the PRS voluntarily. Recording it in the rationale field on the *first* hazard decision means subsequent decisions can cite *standing rule* without re-deriving it.
- **The three reasons for adopting the standing rule** are recorded once. *(i) Tribunal benchmark; (ii) RRA Ombudsman expectations; (iii) pre-empts category-1 becoming category-2 during delay.* These are the three load-bearing arguments a landlord brings to a future Ombudsman review.
- **The outcome records the actual response time.** *[N] days of report; inside voluntary 14-day floor* shows the standing rule was met. A response that breached the standing rule would carry the outcome line *[N] days of report; outside voluntary 14-day floor — see complaint log [N]* and a follow-up entry under COMP.

## When the entry pattern changes

Once Awaab's Law is commenced for the PRS (earliest 2027 per Chapter 4), the rationale field shifts:

- *Statutory duties under [Awaab's Law section reference]; required response within [statutory timetable]. Action taken inside [actual response time].*

The standing rule disappears because the timetable becomes statutory; the discipline of recording *the response time against the timetable* survives.

For category-2 hazards (less severe than category-1), the voluntary standing rule may be relaxed but the entry shape is the same: hazard description, statutory anchor, response taken, resolution date.
