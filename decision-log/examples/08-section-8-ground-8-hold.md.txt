## Why this entry's discipline matters

Section 8 ground-8 service is one of the highest-stakes decisions a landlord makes — a mandatory ground for possession that, if successful at first hearing, ends the tenancy. The decision **not** to serve is also a decision and carries the same documentation discipline.

Three load-bearing details:

- **The NRLA legal helpline call is referenced with date, time, and call reference.** The call note template at [`../../templates/nrla-helpline-summary.md`](../../templates/nrla-helpline-summary.md) captures the substance; the decision-log entry's evidence pointer links to it. A tribunal panel reading this file sees a landlord taking advice before acting, not after.
- **The standing rule is restated.** *Ground-8 service requires solicitor's eye before issue.* This is not a one-off decision; it is the portfolio-wide rule, and the rule's existence is recorded on the entry that invokes it.
- **The cure path is named with a date.** *Back-pay clear date [BACK_PAY_DATE]* and *Section 8 service remains available after that date* are the two halves of the deferral. If back-pay does not arrive by the named date, the next entry in this file would record the decision to serve.

## When the entry pattern reads differently

A decision **to serve** Section 8 ground 8 (rather than hold) carries a longer entry:

- The Form 3A is the human-signed, solicitor-reviewed artefact — referenced in the evidence pointer as `evidence/[tenancy]-form-3a-served-[DD-MM-YYYY].pdf`.
- The rationale field cites the solicitor's letter and the specific ground (8 mandatory; 10 / 11 discretionary; 14 if anti-social-behaviour grounds also apply).
- The outcome field records the possession-claim outcome: *County Court possession order granted [DD/MM/YYYY] under ground 8; possession date [DD/MM/YYYY]* or *Tenant cleared arrears before hearing; claim withdrawn.*

A decision to serve and a decision not to serve sit in the same file — chronological, append-only. The rule against deletion is critical here: the audit trail is the chain.
