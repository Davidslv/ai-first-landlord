## Why this entry's discipline matters

The PRS Ombudsman becomes mandatory 2028; even pre-commencement, complaint handling is the single most contestable area of landlord conduct. The entry shape is the same six-field discipline, with two adjustments:

- **The standing rule is named explicitly.** *All neighbour complaints get a written response inside seven days, an investigation call with the tenant in the same window, and a paper trail that would survive Ombudsman review.* Standing rules pre-empt the *why this and not that* question on every individual entry.
- **The threshold for escalation is recorded.** *Not yet at ground 14 threshold* signals the landlord is monitoring the conduct against the statutory possession ground — a tribunal panel reading this file sees calibration, not over-reaction.

## The "open as at" pattern

This entry is **open** at the time of writing — outcome to be filled at resolution. Three things to know about open entries:

- The quarterly bottleneck-finding pass surfaces any open >90 days. An open complaint entry past 90 days is a flag for review (has the response gone unacknowledged? is the tenant pattern continuing? has the complainant escalated?).
- The outcome field, once filled, names what was decided and on what evidence. *Resolved [DD/MM/YYYY]: tenant complied with midnight cut-off; no further complaints received in the [N]-week monitoring window.* Or: *Resolved [DD/MM/YYYY]: conduct continued; ground 14 threshold met; Section 8 service decision logged in s8-possession.md entry [N].*
- An open entry is not a stalled entry. The audit trail of communication during the open period (tenant call note, written response to complainant, follow-up monitoring emails) all sits under `evidence/` and is referenced from the outcome field at resolution.

## Cross-references to other categories

Complaints often cross into other decision-log categories. A neighbour complaint that becomes Section 8 ground 14 service crosses into S8; a complaint about deposit deductions that goes to scheme adjudication crosses into SELECT (where end-of-tenancy reconciliation lives); a complaint heading toward Ombudsman redress is the substantive COMP entry.

Cross-category decisions go in the most load-bearing category and cross-reference from the others. *Complaint heading to redress* is COMP; the Section 8 service that flows from continued conduct is a separate S8 entry that cross-references back.
