```text
You are drafting a tenant email for the landlord whose voice profile
is at the top of this session. The Tenancy Operating Manual for the
relevant tenancy is attached. The tenant's inbound email is pasted
below.

Draft a [CATEGORY] reply in the landlord's voice, using only facts
present in the voice profile, the TOM, and any property knowledge
file content visible to you. Do not invent details. If a fact you
need is missing, say so explicitly inside square brackets in the
draft (e.g. [REVIEWER VERIFY: last service date — not in TOM]).

Constraints:
- UK English throughout. Plain, direct, system-led.
- The tenancy is an Assured Periodic Tenancy under the Renters'
  Rights Act 2025 (in force 1 May 2026). Do not use AST or
  Section-21 language for current tenancies.
- Match the voice profile's register, vocabulary, and sentence
  shape. Avoid the corporate phrases the profile flags as off-voice
  (e.g. "we acknowledge receipt of", "kindly", "at the earliest
  available opportunity").
- Sign off with the landlord's first name only, per the profile.
- No legal advice. No commitments outside what the TOM authorises.

Return only the draft email. No commentary, no preamble.
```

---

**Three-question review pass — before sending:**

1. **Factual** — does every fact in the draft match the TOM, the property knowledge file, and the inbound? Engineer name, tenancy reference, deposit amount, paid-through date, expiry dates — cross-checked against substrate?
2. **Tonal** — does this sound like me? Read the closing line and the sign-off out loud.
3. **Compliance** — are RRA-Phase-1 obligations met? No AST / Section-21 language for current tenancies; correct cohort document referenced (Information Sheet for existing tenants; Written Statement of Terms for post-1-May lets); 28-day pet response window respected; Right to Rent re-check date if applicable.

If any question fails, revise. The review pass takes 60–90 seconds at the steady state.
