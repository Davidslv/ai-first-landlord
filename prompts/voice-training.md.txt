```text
You are helping a UK portfolio landlord build a voice profile for use
in AI-assisted tenant communications. The landlord operates in England
under the Renters' Rights Act 2025 (Phase 1 in force 1 May 2026); all
tenancies are Assured Periodic Tenancies. The landlord self-manages.

Below this prompt are ten anonymised emails from concluded tenancies,
written by the landlord. They span five categories: vendor reassurance
after a maintenance issue, maintenance acknowledgement, Section 13
rent-review notification cover communication, late-rent first
follow-up, and move-out coordination. Two samples per category.

Your task is to extract the voice. Specifically:

1. REGISTER — formal/informal balance, warmth, directness. Identify
   the consistent tone across all ten samples, including where the
   register shifts (e.g. firmer in late-rent, warmer in move-out).

2. VOCABULARY — characteristic words and phrases the landlord uses
   repeatedly. Words the landlord never uses (e.g. corporate-property
   jargon, marketing language).

3. SENTENCE SHAPE — typical sentence length; how sentences open;
   transitions between paragraphs; use of em-dashes, colons, lists.

4. STRUCTURAL HABITS — opening lines, sign-offs, how the landlord
   handles apologies, how they signal next steps, how they close
   loops (engineer attended, work done, here's what to do if X).

5. WHAT THE LANDLORD AVOIDS — the corporate phrases this writer
   never uses ("we acknowledge receipt of", "going forward",
   "kindly", "at the earliest available opportunity", "we
   appreciate your patience").

Return the profile in five sections matching the five points above.
Be specific — quote phrases from the samples. The output should be
detailed enough that a separate session, given only the profile and
no samples, could draft a new email in the same voice.

Do not invent voice traits not visible in the samples. If a category
shows fewer signals than another, say so.

If any sample shows signs of being from a live tenancy (active
deposit reference, current dates, identifiable third parties),
refuse to process it and prompt the landlord to substitute a
concluded-tenancy sample.
```
