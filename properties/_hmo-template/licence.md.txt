## Renewal trail

- 8 weeks before expiry ([DD/MM/YYYY]): reminder drafted, renewal documents pulled together
- 4 weeks before expiry ([DD/MM/YYYY]): application submitted to [licensing authority], fee paid (£[amount])
- 1 week before expiry ([DD/MM/YYYY]): confirmation chased if not yet received
- On renewal: new licence reference, issue date, expiry date logged; this file's header dates updated

The compliance dashboard reads the **Expiry date** line above and derives the eight-week / four-week / one-week reminders automatically. HMOs also carry a parallel **twelve-week documents-to-refresh** chase to assemble the renewal pack.

## Civil-penalty exposure

Operating an HMO without a required licence carries civil-penalty exposure under section 249A of the Housing Act 2004 — starting-point and maximum bands set by the local authority's policy, escalating for repeated or serious offences. The operational discipline this drives: the renewal trail above is non-negotiable; the eight-week reminder is a hard event in the compliance dashboard, not a soft nudge.
