## OVERRIDES

If the schedule of dilapidations is **likely to surface deductions** (visible damage, smoking-residue concerns, a flagged cleaning issue), do not commit to *ten working days* in the email — the deposit return runs from the day deductions are agreed, not from the inspection date. Replace the deposit line with: *Allow ten working days from the day we agree any deductions, so usually clean by [APPROXIMATE_DATE] if the room is in the same shape it's in today.*

If the outgoing tenant is leaving on **less-than-amicable terms** (deposit dispute pre-emptively flagged, complaint history, mid-tenancy dispute), drop the *household heads-up* paragraph — household communication needs separate handling that does not reference the outgoing tenant by name.

If the outgoing notice is **inside the first 12 months** of the tenancy under post-RRA rules (tenant 2-month notice can be served from day one), the template still fires — there is no minimum-occupation rule under the RRA Phase 1 regime. Do not reference fixed-term break clauses.
