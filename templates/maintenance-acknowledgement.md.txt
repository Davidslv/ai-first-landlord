## VARIABLES

From the inbound email:

- `[ISSUE_SHORT]` — one-line subject ref ("Boiler", "Bathroom radiator")
- `[ISSUE_RECAP]` — verb-phrase from the tenant's email ("it cut out again last night")
- `[FREQUENCY_NOTE]` — landlord's read on the pattern ("twice in a fortnight" / "the third time this year")

From the TOM:

- `[TENANT_FIRST_NAME]`
- `[PROPERTY_REF]` — e.g. *[full address]* / *[address, Room N]*

From the property knowledge file:

- `[SYSTEM]` — boiler / radiator / shower / consumer unit
- `[CONTRACTOR_NAME]` — e.g. the firm on file in baseline.md.txt
- `[ENGINEER_FIRST_NAME]` — e.g. the lead engineer named in baseline.md.txt
- `[CONTRACTOR_PRIOR_CONTEXT]` — one short clause anchoring continuity ("did the annual service in November and remembers the unit")

Set at draft:

- `[ACTION_VERB]` — ring / email / text
- `[TARGET_WINDOW]` — tomorrow / Friday / by midweek
- `[VISIT_DURATION]` — 30–40 minutes / an hour
- `[INTERIM_USE]` — Hot water until then / Use the immersion for now / Avoid the front-room radiator

From the voice profile:

- `[LANDLORD_FIRST_NAME]` — sign-off matches the trained voice

## OVERRIDES

If `[ISSUE_RECAP]` involves a **Category 1 hazard** (gas leak, electrical exposure, no heat in winter for vulnerable tenants), drop the template and ring the tenant. Same-day contractor attendance, not next-day. Cross-refs the Chapter 14 standing rules and the Awaab's-Law-style hazard response when commenced for the PRS.

If the contractor on the property file is unavailable, name the standby explicitly rather than leaving `[CONTRACTOR_NAME]` blank. The tenant reads silence as evasion.
