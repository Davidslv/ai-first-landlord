## VARIABLES

Set at the time of the call:

- `[DD/MM/YYYY HH:MM]` — when the call took place
- `[NRLA-YYYY-NNNNN]` — the call reference the adviser issued
- Your NRLA membership reference
- Caller — usually you, occasionally your bookkeeper or authorised agent

Filled at the time of writing:

- The question, advice, and next-action paragraphs (your prose)
- The primary-source pointers (the adviser's citations)
- The cross-references — only the ones that exist; remove unused lines

## File naming

Suggested: `nrla-call-[YYYY-MM-DD]-[topic-short].md` filed under the relevant decision-log category folder. Examples:

- `decision-log/s8-possession/nrla-call-2026-05-02-davidson-r4-arrears.md`
- `decision-log/pets/nrla-call-2026-04-22-bramwell-cat-permission.md`
- `decision-log/complaints/nrla-call-2026-05-19-glenarm-noise-neighbour.md`

## OVERRIDES

**This template is not a tenant comm.** It is never sent — even where the question concerns a specific tenant. If the advice produces a tenant-facing email, that email is drafted separately (against the relevant scenario template, voice profile, TOM) and references this internal note in the decision log, not in the tenant comm.

**Verbatim advice does not go in the file.** Helpline calls are confidential and the adviser's exact words may not be disclosed without consent. Summarise the substance.

**This is not legal advice on the file.** The adviser is providing helpline guidance, not formal legal advice. Where the matter is high-stakes (Section 8 service, possession proceedings, deposit-protection breach claim), follow up with a property solicitor and record the solicitor's letter separately.
