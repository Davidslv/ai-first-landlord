Last refined: [DD/MM/YYYY]

Subject: Rent received — [PROPERTY_REF] — [PAYMENT_PERIOD]

Hi [TENANT_FIRST_NAME],

Thanks — [PAYMENT_PERIOD] rent of £[RENT_AMOUNT] cleared on
[PAYMENT_DATE]. You're now paid through to [PAID_THROUGH_DATE];

[STANDING_ORDER_NOTE — one of:

  /
  This is the first payment under the new rent — standing order

## VARIABLES

From the bookkeeper / bank reconciliation:

- `[PAYMENT_DATE]` — the date the funds cleared
- `[PAYMENT_PERIOD]` — *October* / *November* / etc.

From the TOM:

- `[TENANT_FIRST_NAME]`
- `[PROPERTY_REF]`
- `[RENT_AMOUNT]` — the live figure (line 6)
- `[PAID_THROUGH_DATE]` — line 7 after this payment
- `[NEXT_DUE_DATE]` — derived from the rent day
- `[PAYMENT_REF]` — the standing-order reference (typically `[PORTFOLIO]-[PROPERTY]-[ROOM]` for HMO rooms)
- `[RENT_DAY]` — the day of the month rent is due
- `[EFFECTIVE_DATE]` — the date the new rent took effect (Section 13 path) or the tenancy start (new let path)

Set at draft:

- `[STANDING_ORDER_NOTE]` — pick one of the three branches: ongoing tenancy with no change, first payment after a rent change, first payment of a new tenancy

From the voice profile:

- `[LANDLORD_FIRST_NAME]`

## OVERRIDES

If the payment cleared **short** (partial payment), do not fire this template. Drop to [`late-rent-first-chase.md.txt`](late-rent-first-chase.md.txt) and acknowledge the partial inside that frame.

If the payment cleared after a Section 13 rent change but at the **old amount** (tenant's standing order not updated), this template still fires for the part received — append a short note that the difference is now outstanding and needs a one-off transfer plus a standing-order amendment from the next cycle.
