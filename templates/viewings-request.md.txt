```text
TEMPLATE: viewings-request.md.txt
Scenario: Mid-tenancy viewings — typically in the last fortnight
          before move-out for a re-let, or as a one-off for a
          refinance valuation. Names the proposed window; offers
          reschedule; respects the right to refuse unreasonable
          times. Two clustered afternoons is the standing pattern
          rather than rolling one-by-one.
Last refined: [DD/MM/YYYY]
Seeded from approved drafts: [D-REF-1], [D-REF-2], [D-REF-3]

---

Subject: Viewings for the new tenancy — [PROPERTY_REF]

Hi [TENANT_FIRST_NAME(S)],

I'm planning to advertise [PROPERTY_REF] from [LISTING_DATE] —
which means I'd like to do viewings inside your last fortnight,
ideally in two afternoon clusters so it's one disruption rather
than five.

Proposing:

  - [CLUSTER_1_DATE], [CLUSTER_1_WINDOW] (six 30-minute slots)
  - [CLUSTER_2_DATE], [CLUSTER_2_WINDOW] (six 30-minute slots)

Each viewing is 30 minutes; I'm with every applicant for the
walk; the place doesn't need to be tidy beyond the obvious. If
either afternoon doesn't work — Saturday family things, you're
away — say so and I'll find a different slot. I won't book any
viewings outside the two windows above without checking with
you first.

[LANDLORD_FIRST_NAME]
```

```text
works for them; whichever fits you better is fine. I'll be there
to let them in — no need for you to be in.

[LANDLORD_FIRST_NAME]
```

## OVERRIDES

If the tenant has **explicitly opted out** of viewings during their notice period (some agreements carry a quiet-enjoyment clause that constrains mid-tenancy viewings), do not fire this template. The decision-log entry for the next-tenant timing goes under `decision-log/selection.md` with the alternative arrangement (post-move-out viewings only — accept the void week).

If the tenancy is **in dispute** (deposit-deduction pre-emptively flagged, complaint heading toward redress), do not fire this template — viewings during a contested move-out add tone-friction the system shouldn't compound. Manual handling.

If you are using a **let-only agency** for the re-let (not self-managed), drop the template — the agent handles their own viewings booking and you should not also be requesting from the tenant.

The **right-to-refuse-unreasonable-times** principle under post-RRA frameworks means evening, early-morning, or late-night slots should not be the default ask. The two-afternoon-cluster pattern keeps viewings within reasonable hours.
